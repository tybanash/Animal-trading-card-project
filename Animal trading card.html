Mandrill

Mandrills are found in the western and central parts of Africa. Their number has been drastically reduced in the last couple of decades. This is due to habitat loss as agriculture and deforestation increases yearly. Therefore, they are classified as vulnerable..

  • Scientific Name: Mandrillus sphinx
  • Average Length: Male:75-95cm and female: 55-66cm
  • Average Lifespan: 20years
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforests located on flat plateaus and mountain areas

Mandrills have unique body coloration which makes them more beautiful as compared to other monkeys. Their faces have red and blue marks. Brightly colored behinds are sign of sexual maturity. They also reflect the emotional state of the animal: color is more pronounced when animal is excited.
